## Why Genre Matters

D&D defaults to **heroic fantasy**, but campaigns can blend genres:
- Horror-fantasy (Ravenloft-style)
- Noir-fantasy (detective urban campaign)
- Pirate-fantasy (swashbuckling high seas)
- Political-fantasy (Game of Thrones-style)
- Comedy-fantasy (lighthearted parody)
- Weird West-fantasy (frontier magic)

Genre determines:
- Tone and mood
- Threat types
- Success conditions
- Player expectations

## Pure Heroic Fantasy (Default)

Standard D&D heroic fantasy genre.

**Core Elements**:
- Heroes overcome evil through courage and strength
- Magic is powerful but structured
- Clear good vs. evil (mostly)
- Victory through combat and cleverness
- Optimistic tone (heroes can win)

**Conventions**:
- PCs are heroes (or become them)
- Villains can be defeated
- Dire consequences can be avoided
- The world is worth saving

**Emotional Range**: 
- Triumph, adventure, discovery
- Occasional tragedy, but hope prevails

**Load Standard Files**: All default Architect files support heroic fantasy.

## Horror-Fantasy Hybrid

Blend horror elements with fantasy:

**Core Elements**:
- Dread and suspense
- Uncomfortable situations
- Consequences often unavoidable
- Victory may be survival, not triumph
- Darker tone (heroes might fail)

**Horror Subgenres**:
- **Gothic Horror**: Curses, haunted places, decay
- **Cosmic Horror**: Incomprehensible entities, insignificance
- **Body Horror**: Transformation, mutation, infection
- **Psychological Horror**: Madness, paranoia, betrayal

**Design Adjustments**:

**NPCs**: 
- More Haunted Voice Register
- Tragic trajectories
- Unreliable or doomed

**Locations**:
- Emphasize decay and wrongness
- Sensory horror (smell of rot, sounds in dark)
- Trapped or isolated settings

**Creatures**:
- Uncanny and unsettling
- Powers that corrupt or transform
- Fates worse than death

**Information**:
- Some knowledge better not known
- Mysteries that horrify when revealed
- Restricted/Hidden tiers more prominent

**Emotional Pacing**:
- Higher tension baseline
- Fear as primary emotion
- Victory feels costly

**Session Structure**:
- Longer tension builds
- Dread before reveals
- Rest beats feel uneasy

Tag: `[GENRE: Horror-Fantasy ‚Äî dread, costly victories, darker tone, Haunted voice register]`

### Horror Guidelines

**Do**:
- Build suspense before horror
- Use unknown and unseen
- Make PCs feel vulnerable
- Respect player boundaries (safety tools)

**Don't**:
- Constant jump scares (exhausting)
- Horror without breaks (burnout)
- Remove all agency (frustrating)
- Ignore safety boundaries

Tag: `[HORROR BALANCE: build suspense, allow breaks, respect boundaries]`

## Noir-Fantasy Hybrid

Detective/mystery blend with fantasy:

**Core Elements**:
- Moral ambiguity (no clear good/evil)
- Urban settings, city underbelly
- Mystery and investigation focus
- Cynical tone (everyone has secrets)
- Information is currency

**Design Adjustments**:

**NPCs**:
- Everyone lies or has agenda
- Motivations complex and selfish
- Trust is rare and valuable

**Factions**:
- Corrupt or compromised
- Gray morality (no pure good)
- Alliances temporary and pragmatic

**Information Systems**:
- Crucial to genre
- ACCESS PATHS emphasize investigation
- Restricted/Hidden tiers highly valuable

**Moral Systems**:
- Moral Consequence Register central
- No clear "right" choices
- Shades of gray, not black/white

**Locations**:
- Urban, often dark and rain-soaked
- Backrooms, alleys, seedy taverns
- Atmosphere of decay and corruption

**Emotional Range**:
- Cynicism, distrust, disillusionment
- Occasional hope, but tempered
- Victory feels incomplete

Tag: `[GENRE: Noir-Fantasy ‚Äî moral ambiguity, investigation focus, urban corruption, cynical tone]`

## Political-Fantasy Hybrid

Game of Thrones-style intrigue:

**Core Elements**:
- Power struggles between factions
- Intrigue, betrayal, alliances
- Social consequences as important as combat
- Long-term planning and strategy
- Morally gray characters

**Design Adjustments**:

**Factions**:
- Central to genre
- Complex relationship webs
- Power dynamics constantly shifting

**Social Systems**:
- Leverage and debt crucial
- Reputation and favor economy
- Social consequences of every action

**NPCs**:
- Politically motivated
- Hidden agendas
- Shifting loyalties

**Combat**:
- Less frequent
- When happens, highly consequential
- Assassination and intrigue common

**Information**:
- Secrets are weapons
- Spying and intelligence gathering
- Information tiers as leverage

**Temporal Systems**:
- Living Clock for multiple factions
- Long-term faction trajectories
- Between-session political shifts

Tag: `[GENRE: Political-Fantasy ‚Äî faction intrigue, social consequences, power struggles, shifting alliances]`

## Pirate-Fantasy Hybrid

Swashbuckling high seas adventure:

**Core Elements**:
- Naval combat and ship-to-ship
- Treasure hunting and exploration
- Freedom and rogues' code
- Larger-than-life personalities
- Banter and swagger

**Design Adjustments**:

**NPCs**:
- Boisterous and theatrical
- Colloquial speech registers
- Treasure-motivated

**Locations**:
- Ships, islands, port cities
- Coastal and aquatic focus
- Exotic locales

**Factions**:
- Pirate crews, navies, trading companies
- Territory = sea routes and ports
- Reputation systems prominent

**Combat**:
- Swashbuckling style (acrobatics, wit)
- Naval encounters
- Boarding actions

**Rewards**:
- Treasure focus
- Ships and maritime gear
- Reputation as legendary pirates

**Voice Register**:
- Nautical terminology
- Predatory (pirates) or Formal (navy)

Tag: `[GENRE: Pirate-Fantasy ‚Äî naval focus, treasure hunting, swashbuckling combat, maritime setting]`

## Comedy-Fantasy Hybrid

Lighthearted, humorous tone:

**Core Elements**:
- Humor and absurdity
- Parody of fantasy tropes
- Stakes present but not oppressive
- Silliness and fun encouraged
- Optimistic and lighthearted

**Design Adjustments**:

**NPCs**:
- Exaggerated personalities
- Comic foibles and quirks
- Self-aware of tropes

**Situations**:
- Absurd complications
- Comedic misunderstandings
- Pratfalls and physical comedy

**Tone**:
- Never grimdark
- Victories are fun and celebratory
- Failures are funny, not tragic

**Emotional Scarcity**:
- Tragedy very rare (if ever)
- Mostly joy, surprise, laughter

**Combat**:
- Less lethal (narratively)
- Humorous descriptions
- Silly enemy tactics

**Restrictions**:
- Avoid serious trauma
- Keep "Prohibited Behaviors" strict
- Player discomfort = stop immediately

Tag: `[GENRE: Comedy-Fantasy ‚Äî humor focus, lighthearted, parody tropes, avoid trauma]`

## Weird West-Fantasy Hybrid

Frontier setting with magic:

**Core Elements**:
- Wild frontier towns and wilderness
- Guns and magic coexist
- Lawless or frontier justice
- Exploration and gold rush
- Cultural mixing (many factions meeting)

**Design Adjustments**:

**Locations**:
- Frontier towns, saloons, open plains
- Native ruins with magic
- Mining camps and railways

**NPCs**:
- Gunslingers, sheriffs, prospectors
- Native peoples with magic
- Outlaws and pioneers

**Tech Level**:
- Guns common (work with magic)
- Trains and telegraphs
- Mix of low and higher tech

**Factions**:
- Outlaw gangs, law enforcers, native tribes
- Mining companies, railway barons
- Complex cultural tensions

**Voice Register**:
- Western colloquialisms
- Frontier slang
- Cultural linguistic variety

**Combat**:
- Gunfights and duels
- Quick draws
- Tactical cover-based

Tag: `[GENRE: Weird West-Fantasy ‚Äî frontier setting, guns + magic, frontier justice, cultural mixing]`

## Genre Blend Maintenance

When mixing genres, maintain both:

### The Genre Balance Rule

**Track Genre Threads**:
```
**Genre Balance:**
- **Primary Genre**: [genre] ‚Äî [% of content] ‚Äî [key elements]
- **Secondary Genre**: [genre] ‚Äî [% of content] ‚Äî [key elements]

Example:
- Primary: Heroic Fantasy ‚Äî 60% ‚Äî Combat, exploration, heroism
- Secondary: Horror ‚Äî 40% ‚Äî Dread, suspense, cosmic threat
```

**Maintain Both**:
- Don't let one genre overwhelm
- Alternate genre emphasis by session or arc
- Use genre clash for effect

### Genre Thread Tracking

```

```

**Balance Over Time**: Average should match intended blend.

## Genre Clash for Effect

Sometimes mixing creates powerful moments:

**Examples**:
- **Comedy ‚Üí Horror**: Lighthearted scene suddenly dark (shocking)
- **Horror ‚Üí Heroic**: After dread, moment of triumph (cathartic)
- **Political ‚Üí Combat**: Intrigue explodes into violence (climactic)
- **Noir ‚Üí Comedy**: Brief absurd moment in cynical world (relief)

**Use Sparingly**: Clash is powerful but can confuse if constant.

## Player Expectations and Genre

Set genre expectations at Session Zero:

**Discuss**:
- What genre(s) are we playing?
- What tone should campaign have?
- What elements do players want/avoid?
- How dark or light?
- How much comedy vs. seriousness?

**Mismatched Expectations**:
- Player expects heroic, gets horror ‚Üí uncomfortable
- Player expects comedy, gets grimdark ‚Üí dissatisfied
- Player expects political, gets combat-only ‚Üí bored

**Solution**: Explicitly state genre blend and get buy-in.

## Genre-Specific Content Warnings

Different genres need different safety tools:

**Horror**:
- Body horror, gore, psychological trauma
- Cosmic insignificance themes
- Inescapable doom

**Noir**:
- Moral ambiguity, corruption
- No clear "good guys"
- Cynical worldview

**Political**:
- Betrayal, intrigue
- Power imbalance
- Difficult moral choices

**Comedy**:
- Embarrassment, silly situations
- Parody might offend (trope subversion)

**Discuss boundaries at Session Zero** (see [43-EMOTIONAL-SYSTEMS.md](43-EMOTIONAL-SYSTEMS.md)).

Genre affects clock speed:

**Horror**: Slower build, sudden acceleration in Stage 3-4
**Noir**: Slow burn, multiple clocks low-intensity
**Political**: Very slow, many clocks simultaneously
**Pirate**: Fast-paced, 3-stage clocks
**Comedy**: Variable, depends on tone

## Genre Drift

Sometimes campaigns shift genre over time:

**Planned Drift**:
- Start lighthearted (comedy), become serious (heroic)
- Start heroic, descend into horror
- Political intrigue escalates to war (combat focus)

**Unplanned Drift**:
- Players prefer different genre than started
- DM interest shifts
- Story naturally evolves

**Managing Drift**:
- Acknowledge shift with players
- Check if everyone comfortable
- Adjust worldbuilding approach
- Update genre tags
